Quantum Flash Drives

Many non-volatile memories (including types of Flash memory) use floating gate transistors like this one. Like many transistors, it has three important terminals: the source, the drain and the control gate. In a normal (non-floating) transistor applying a voltage on the gate will create a channel between the source and the drain that allows current to flow between the source and drain.

Quantum Tunneling

Quantum tunnelling, also known as tunneling (US) is a quantum mechanical phenomenon whereby a wavefunction can propagate through a potential barrier. The transmission through the barrier can be finite and depends exponentially on the barrier height and barrier width.
